2015 in review

A year of big changes and even bigger adventures.

New year. New job. New state.

First of all, welcome to my version of "year end holiday car update about Shawn". As some of you may already know, last year was a huge year for me. It included a big move across the country as well as a bunch of exciting family events.

Silicon Valley

Probably the biggest change for me in 2015 was the move out to California for my new job. 2015 marked 3 years with Sogeti and I decided that it was time to move on and see what else was out there. During a trip to California to visit a good friend, I was introduced to Dynamic Signal and proceeded to pursue a career out in Silicon Valley. The move wasn't easy as it put me thousands of miles away from my family but they have been nothing but supportive and the impact it has had on my career has been outstanding.

"Hey bro, let me tell you about my startup..."

I currently live in Palo Alto, home of Stanford University, and about 30 minutes south of San Francisco. Since I don't need one, I got rid of my car in favor or riding CalTrain to get around, which is a huge change from what I was used to in Indiana

Weddings and such

This year I was honored to be in my brother's wedding. The weather was beautifully hot and it was really awesome to see the two families come together and celebrate.

Misc Adventures

Yosemite

Before I moved out to California, I came out to simply visit Madison and take a trip to Yosemite. We went hiking all day on Four Mile Trail and got to see stunning views of Yosemite Falls, El Capitan, and Half Dome. It was an amazing trip and I encourage everyone to visit there at least once during their lifetime.

Madison and I in Yosemite
A view from above Yosemite

The Lost Coast

When I moved to California, I had about a week before I started work and managed to participate in an extended backpacking trip to the Lost Coast with a bunch of friends over 4th of July weekend. Over the course of a couple days we hiked across miles of blackened beaches, saw a beached whale, climbed King's Range up a ridiculously steep incline to the other side where it was blistering hot, and then came back down the other side. The scenery was absolutely beautiful. Pictures don't do it justice but they certainly help give you an idea.

A look back at the beach we just finished hiking on the Lost Coast
A view for royalty atop King's Peak

Memphis

For Thanksgiving, I traveled with Madison to her hometown of Memphis, Tennesee. While we were there I was treated with unparalled southern hospitality, tons of barbeque, drinking until the sun came up, good cooking, and endless baked goods from Muddy's Bake Shop. We visited the Peabody, Memphis University, the house where she grew up, her dad's shop, the Lorraine Hotel, and much more. I had a blast and look forward to going back.

Carmel & Big Sur

Considering the expense of moving across the country, I wasn't able to spend the holidays in Indiana this year. Thankfully we live in a day in age where we can FaceTime with our loved ones across the globe in real-time. This is exactly what I did this past holiday season from Carmel, California. Carmel is a quaint town near Monterrey and Big Sur. Madison and I spent the week dog-sitting for some friends and enjoying the beaches and amazing surroundings including the Esalen Hot Springs.
